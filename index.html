APG Major Incident Classification Tool

This form is designed to classify the incident based on DORA criteria. The information submitted through the form is neither stored nor processed.

Question 1: Does the incident affect Critical Services?

Assess if the incident affects one of the below options:

  • a) Affects or has affected ICT services or network and information systems that support critical or important functions of the financial entity. This refers to any disruption of business-critical processes or critical incidents affecting key business applications identified as essential for the continuity of APG's operations.
  • b) Affects or has affected financial services that require authorisation, registration, or are supervised by competent authorities. Scenario 1 (Authorization): An asset manager experiences a system failure that prevents clients from placing trades in a timely manner. This could affect the asset manager’s ability to meet its obligations to clients and may violate the authorization conditions imposed by the relevant financial regulator (e.g., the firm is required to ensure timely execution of trades).
    Scenario 2 (Registration): An asset manager's failure to update or report the status of a registered financial product or fund to the regulatory authority could trigger compliance violations, potentially leading to fines or operational restrictions.
    Scenario 3 (Supervision): An incident like a data breach could affect the asset manager’s compliance with data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR) or other regulatory requirements that are overseen by the competent authorities. This could lead to regulatory investigations and reputational damage.
  • c) Represents a successful, malicious, and unauthorised access to the network and information systems of the financial entity. This refers to any unauthorized cyber attack, data breach, or other forms of security compromise.


Question 2: Is there a security breach?

Additional Impact Assessment Questions

Question 3A: Affected Clients, financial counterparts, and transactions

Does the incident meet any of the following conditions?
a) the number of affected clients is higher than 10% of all clients using the affected service; or
b) the number of affected clients is higher than 100,000 clients using the affected service; or
c) the number of affected financial counterparts is higher than 30% of all financial counterparts carrying out activities related to the provision of the affected service; or
d) the number of affected transactions is higher than 10% of the daily average number of transactions carried out by the financial entity related to the affected service; or
e) the amount of affected transactions is higher than 10% of the daily average value of transactions carried out by the financial entity related to the affected service; or
f) any identified impact on clients or financial counterparts which have been identified as relevant as an outcome of the assessment made by the financial entity under Article 1(3).


Question 3B: Data losses

Does the incident have any impact on the availability, authenticity, integrity or confidentiality of data, which has or will have an adverse impact on the implementation of the business objectives of the Financial Entity or on meeting regulatory requirements?
1. availability of data – data on demand rendered temporarily or permanently inaccessible or unusable;
2. authenticity of data – compromised trustworthiness of the source of data;
3. integrity of data – data inaccurate or incomplete due to non-authorised modification;
4. confidentiality of data – data being accessed by or disclosed to an unauthorised party or system.


Question 3C: Reputational impact

Is there any reputational impact?
Reputational impact evidenced by any of the below:
a) incident reflected in the media; or
b) received repetitive complaints; or
c) inability to meet regulatory requirements; or
d) likely loss of clients or financial counterparts with a material impact on FE's business. Level of visibility of the incident to be taken into account.


Question 3D: Duration and Service Downtime

Is the a) incident duration longer than 24 hours;
or b) service downtime longer than 2 hours for ICT services that support critical or important functions
1. Duration measured from the moment an incident occurs or is detected, until it is resolved. (estimate if not yet known)
2. Service downtime measured from the moment service fully/partially unavailable/delayed to clients, financial counterparts or other internal or external users, until activities are restored to the same level before the incident.


Question 3E: Geographical Spread

Is there any impact of the incident identified in the territories of at least two EU Member States?
Assess significant impact of the incident in other EU Member States on:
a) clients or financial counterparts;
b) branches of the FE or other group financial entities;
c) Financial market infrastructures or third-party providers that may affect other FEs.


Question 3F: Economic Impact

Do the costs and losses incurred by APG exceed or are likely to exceed €100,000 (can be based on estimates where actuals cannot be determined)?
Types of direct and indirect incurred costs:
a) expropriated funds or financial assets liability, including theft;
b) replacement or relocation costs;
c) staff costs;
d) contract non-compliance fees;
e) customer redress and compensation costs;
f) forgone revenues;
g) communication costs;
h) advisory costs. (based on available data at the time of reporting)
